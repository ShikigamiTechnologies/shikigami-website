The operating problem

The owner should not be the company’s human integration layer.

Email, field photos, customer requests, vendor promises, documents, and internal follow-up arrive in different places. IronCrew creates one controlled operating loop without removing human authority.

01

Collect

Bring approved communication, forms, documents, comments, and field evidence into a unified operations inbox.

02

Understand

Classify the project, topic, urgency, requested action, deadline, and supporting records.

03

Coordinate

Prepare work, assignments, follow-ups, briefings, and audience-specific drafts from verified context.

04

Decide

Route consequential actions to the right authorized person with evidence, impact, and confidence.

AI with explicit authority

Useful enough to reduce workload. Bounded enough to trust.

Every automation has an authority level. Low-confidence work stops for review, and consequential communication cannot silently become an action.

OBSERVESummarize and classify

Safe visibility without changing operational records.

RECOMMENDPropose the next step

Show evidence, confidence, and likely impact.

DRAFTPrepare communication

Create a reviewable response from verified project context.

APPROVALAuthorized person confirms

Required for pricing, contracts, schedules, disputes, safety, personnel, and legal language.

AUTO-EXECUTELow-risk and reversible only

Limited to pre-approved routine actions with audit history.

ESCALATEStop when uncertainty is material

Route exceptions instead of guessing.

Measured operational value

Reduce administrative preparation—not the owner’s judgment.

IronCrew is designed to reduce reading, searching, drafting, routing, reporting, and follow-up while preserving control over financial, contractual, safety, customer, and personnel decisions.

80%target reduction in email reading and sorting
90%target reduction in manual follow-up and reminders
85%target reduction in meeting documentation
0–25%target reduction in high-impact decisions

Targets require a measured customer baseline and controlled pilot. They are objectives—not guaranteed results.

Controlled pilot

Start with one real operating loop.

We begin with a bounded organization, approved users, synthetic or authorized operational data, and measurable acceptance criteria. Staging validation is active; public production activation remains gated by final infrastructure, recovery, mailbox, and owner acceptance.

No public self-signupHuman approval for outbound actionsConsequential decisions remain human

A pilot defines

  • Owner workload baseline
  • One inbox and work-routing flow
  • Safe and approval-required message classes
  • Accuracy, citation, and draft acceptance thresholds
  • Tenant, recovery, security, and rollback evidence
